Here are my latest studies (my research experiences can be seen here):

  • Utterance-final high vowel diphthongization in Chongqing Mandarin.
    Boundary floating moras enforce utterance-final diphthongization on high vowels in (C)V syllables in Chongqing Mandarin, where newly generated quality can be derived by OT constraints of OCP and Prominence Alignment. [pdf in prep] [abstract and slides for 30th MFM]
  • Learnability of /g/ nasalization in compounds in Japanese and an artificial language.
    The unnatural length-referencing alternation of Japanese /g/ nasalization is under-learned by native speakers (the surfeit of the stimulus), as evidenced by a wug test [poster] and (a hypothesis to be verified is that) there is a gradient universal learning bias against it, as evidenced by an artificial grammar learning experiment [in prep].
