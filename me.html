About me

My name is Hailang Jiang [haɪ.lɑŋ d͜ʒʲɑŋ] (Mandarin: 江 海浪 [t͜ɕʲɑ́ŋ xài.lɑ̂ŋ]). Hailang means ‘sea wave’ in Chinese. (Actually ‘jiang’ means ‘river’; you may find these three characters all contain the meaning-indicating radical ‘氵’ – three drops of water!) You are free to call me Hailang, or ‘Lang-lang’ the reduplicative form, just like my friends.

I grew up in Chongqing, a city in Southwestern China. I received my undergraduate education at Peking University in Beijing, China, majoring in Economics. Before phonology, I once had an interest in microeconomics and game theory.

Now I am studying in the Master program in Linguistics (Specializing in Phonology) offered by UCL in London, UK.

In my leisure time, I am 'p-side' as well; p for photography, painting and papercraft (e.g., modelling, quilling, folding, but never writing!). I take photos of city views, nature and portraits. I enjoy hiking and traveling when I don’t have to work to a deadline. I relish composing diaries, prose, and poetry in my mother tongue. I am enamored with the season when leaves turn yellow and red, then flutter down one after another, every year.
