Here are my latest studies:

  • Boundary floating moras enforce utterance-final diphthongization on monomoraic syllables with high vowels in Chongqing Mandarin. [pdf] [slides for 30th MFM]
    Abstract: In Chongqing Mandarin, there is an alternation that high vowels undergo utterance-finally where they are diphthongized. This paper presents an analysis of diphthongization as fission driven by the pressure for boundary floating morasutterance-final syllables to be attaches to monomoraic syllables with high vowels. Utterance-final high vowels are not normally lengthened due to general phonotactic constraints, and we are able to derive the similarity of the second parts in diphthongs which do not show up anywhere else in this language. This is a case of 'emergence of the unmarked'.
