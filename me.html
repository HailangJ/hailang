About me

My name is Hailang Jiang [haɪ.lɑŋ d͜ʒɑŋ][1] (Mandarin: 江 海浪 [t͜ɕʲɑ́ŋ xài.lɑ̂ŋ]). Hailang means ‘sea wave’ in Chinese[2]. You are free to call me Hailang, or ‘Lang-lang’ the reduplicative form[3], just like my friends.

I grew up in Chongqing, a city in Southwestern China. I received my undergraduate education at Peking University in Beijing, majoring in Economics. Before phonology, I once had an interest in microeconomics and game theory.

Now I am studying in the Master program in Linguistics (with a Specialization in Phonology) offered by UCL in London, UK.

During my leisurely moments, I am ‘p-side’ as well; p for photography, painting and papercraft (namely, paper modelling, quilling, folding, but never writing). I take photos of cityscapes, nature and people. I enjoy hiking and traveling when I don’t have to work to a deadline. I relish composing diaries, prose, and poetry in my mother tongue. I am enamored with the season when leaves turn yellow and red, then flutter down one after another, every year.


  1. This is identical to what is spelled as High-Long Jong in American English. Note that *[haɪ.læŋ d͜ʒæŋ], as indicated by the spelling of my name, sounds weird to me which is more like ‘Hailan Jian’, because Mandarin speakers use vowels’ backness as the main cue to distinguish between an [an]/[æn] and ang [ɑŋ], not the coda.
  2. And Jiang means ‘river’; you may find these three characters all contain the radical ‘氵’ three drops of water, which always indicates the meaning of water.
  3. In my native dialect (Chongqing Mandarin), the fully reduplicative form of certain syllables used as the last syllable in one’s given name is widely used as a nickname.
