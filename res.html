My research experiences (papers and conference presentations)

Utterance-final high vowel diphthongization in Chongqing Mandarin.
  • Jiang, Hailang (2023). Utterance-final high vowel diphthongization in Chongqing Mandarin. Talk presented at the 30th Manchester Phonology Meeting, University of Manchester. [abstract and slides]
  • Jiang, Hailang (2023). Utterance-final high vowel diphthongization in Chongqing Mandarin: an HS account. To be submitted. [pdf]
Abstract: Boundary floating moras enforce utterance-final diphthongization on high vowels in (C)V syllables in Chongqing Mandarin, where newly generated quality can be derived by OT constraints of OCP and Prominence Alignment. [pdf]
Learning voice velar nasalization in compounds in Standard Japanese.
  • Jiang, Hailang (2023). The learnability of the mora-counting alternation of voiced velar nasalization in Japanese. Poster presented at Poster Session of the 2023 LSA Summer Institute, University of Massachusetts, Amherst. [poster] [pdf]
  • Jiang, Hailang (2023). An analytic bias in learning length-referencing alternations. Master dissertation, University College London. [pdf]
Joint Abstract: The unnatural length-referencing alternation of Japanese voiced velar nasalization is under-learned by native speakers (the surfeit of the stimulus), as evidenced by a wug test and there is a universal learning bias against it, as evidenced by an artificial grammar learning experiment.
