Here are my latest studies (my research experience can be seen here):

  • Utterance-final high vowel diphthongization in Chongqing Mandarin.
    Boundary floating moras enforce utterance-final diphthongization on high vowels in (C)V syllables in Chongqing Mandarin, where newly generated quality can be derived by OT constraints of OCP and Prominence Alignment. [pdf] [abstract and slides for 30th MFM]
  • Learning voice velar nasalization in compounds in Standard Japanese.
    The unnatural length-referencing alternation of Japanese voiced velar nasalization is under-learned by native speakers (the surfeit of the stimulus), as evidenced by a wug test [pdf] and there is a universal learning bias against it, as evidenced by an artificial grammar learning experiment [this part is my master’s degree dissertation, pdf].
